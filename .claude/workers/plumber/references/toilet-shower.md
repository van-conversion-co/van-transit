# Toilet and Shower Decision Guide

Use when the owner raises toilet or shower requirements. Ask explicitly — neither is a default assumption.

---

## Shower

Ask explicitly whether the owner wants a shower. If yes:

- A shower requires a grey tank — cannot be omitted if showering inside the van.
- The grey tank must be mounted equidistant between sink and shower drain at sufficient height drop for gravity drainage from both.
- A shower cubicle or "wet room" takes significant space — confirm zone position with Architect before plumbing is designed. The Carpenter will need to waterproof the floor and walls of the wet room zone.
- Flag the shower zone as a constraint in Decisions.md with `blocks_worker: Architect` before layout is finalised.

**Outdoor shower alternative**: a portable solar shower or hose from a tank mounted on the roof is a valid no-space-cost option for warmer climates or stealth camping — worth surfacing to the owner before committing to an indoor installation.

---

## Toilet

39% of conversions include a toilet — it is not a default assumption. Ask explicitly if the owner wants one. If yes, establish which type:

| Type | Smell | Emptying | Space cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Portable cassette | Strong chemical smell | Regular; designated points needed | Low — small, movable |
| Composting | Slight earthy scent | Chemicals disposed as regular waste | Medium — fixed location needed |
| Full cassette/wet | Low if sealed | Regular at motorhome services | High |

A toilet takes meaningful storage/living space — confirm the owner has accounted for this in the layout before specifying one.

**Detailed toilet type comparison:**

| | Portable | Composting | DIY composting |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cost | £100+ | £300–£1,000 | £100–£300 |
| Emptying | Not ideal — chemical disposal points only | Not too bad | Not too bad |
| Frequency | Once a week | Liquids every 2–4 days; solids ~monthly | Similar to composting |
| Size | Medium | Large | Medium |
| Smell | Chemical | Earthy | Earthy |
| Example | Dometic 972 | Nature's Head (£1,000) | Kildwick EasyLoo (~£250 components) |

**Composting toilet mechanics**: separates liquids (front tank) and solids (rear tank covered with peat moss, coconut coir, or sawdust). A churning handle mixes the solids with the medium to reduce smell. A built-in fan removes evaporating liquids and vents odours outside. The urine tank must be emptied every 2–4 days; the solid waste tank roughly monthly. When using a composting toilet, the owner must sit down — keeping liquids and solids separate is essential to minimising smell.

**DIY composting option**: kits such as the Kildwick EasyLoo provide components to build your own composting toilet for ~£250, taking up less space than a pre-built unit. Worth suggesting to space-constrained builds.

Log the toilet type as a Decisions.md entry.
