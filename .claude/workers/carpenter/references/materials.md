# Material Selection

Use when deciding what to build with. Material choice has a large impact on weight, cost, and durability.

---

## Recommended

- **12mm birch ply** — the clear winner for van builds. Good strength-to-weight ratio, holds screws well, widely available, moisture-resistant when sealed. 12mm is the standard thickness for furniture carcasses.
- **Bamboo** — very light and strong but can be hard to source at reasonable price. Use sparingly.
- **Hardwoods** — strong but heavy; use sparingly (visible trim, edges only).

## Avoid

- **MDF, particleboard, OSB** — poor strength-to-weight ratio, soak up water, do not hold screws well. Acceptable only if completely sealed on all sides and edges. Avoid unless the owner has a specific reason.

---

## Weight comparison — this matters

An L-shaped kitchen built with standard 18mm MDF domestic units weighs approximately **35kg more** than the same kitchen built from 12mm birch ply. Domestic IKEA-style units use 18mm MDF carcasses — 25% heavier than redwood ply relative to strength. Always check the weight implication against Van.md payload headroom before recommending domestic units.

---

## Domestic vs campervan vs bespoke units

| Option | Weight | Cost | Fit | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Domestic units (IKEA, B&Q) | Heavy (18mm MDF) | Cheap | Poor — van walls are not perpendicular | Will not fit flush without modification |
| Campervan-specific pre-built | Lighter | Higher | Designed for van dimensions | Caravan aesthetic; only practical for small–medium vans |
| Bespoke from 12mm birch ply | Best weight | Moderate (materials + time) | Perfect | Best weight, fit, and finish — requires more skill and time |

Bespoke bespoke birch ply builds give the best outcome for weight, fit, and finish. Domestic units are a reasonable shortcut for budget builds if the weight impact is within payload headroom.
