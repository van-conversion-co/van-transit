# Exterior Planning Checks

Run before interior layout begins. Every exterior feature must be located and decided before any interior work starts — retrofitting after cladding means chopping through insulation, vapour barrier, and cladding. Log each confirmed position as a Decisions.md entry.

---

## Checklist

- Windows and roof lights (positions confirmed before shell prep)
- Roof fan / skylight aperture (see Roof Penetration Planning below)
- Solar panel positions and cable entry gland location — if the roof is significantly curved, flag to Electrician that flexible panels may be the only option (affects efficiency and lifespan expectations)
- Water fill point (exterior) and gas fill point (exterior) positions
- Any fridge vent, boiler flue, or combustion appliance vent
- WiFi booster antenna or TV aerial (easy to overlook until living in the van — plan the cable route now)
- Reversing camera (especially important on large vans)
- Awning bar fixing points (require interior access to attach from inside)
- Roof rack fixing points (same — require interior access)

All positions must be confirmed before the shell phase begins.

---

## Roof Penetration Planning

Before any layout is finalised, identify all planned roof penetrations — roof fan, vent, aerial, solar cable entry gland. For each: log a candidate Decisions.md entry with `Irreversible: Yes` and `blocks_worker: Builder`. Roof fan position must be confirmed before cladding is planned, as it affects panel layout, internal headroom at that point, wiring runs, and the Builder's insulation sequence. Record confirmed position in Van.md under 'Existing holes / planned penetrations'.

## Multiple Roof Features — Compatibility Check

If solar panels, roof rack, and skylight/roof fan are all in scope together, check for conflicts. A standard roof rack may obstruct solar panels or block a skylight; a custom rack or specific positioning may be needed. Multiple roof cable entries (solar, aerials, WiFi) should ideally share a single cable entry housing to minimise penetrations and leak risk. Surface conflicts before any cut is made.

## Pop-Top Roof

If the owner is considering a pop-top (relevant mainly for small vans wanting stand-up headroom), flag that cutting out a large roof section significantly weakens the van's structural rigidity. The installation requires additional structural strengthening beams as part of the process. This is a major irreversible modification — log as Decisions.md entry with `Irreversible: Yes` and note that professional fabrication is strongly recommended unless the owner has relevant fabrication experience. A pop-top also creates a long perimeter seam that must be watertight.
